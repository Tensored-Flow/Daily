## Top 10 Developments

**1. EU Digital Omnibus: high-risk assessment-AI obligations on track to slip from Aug 2, 2026 to Dec 2, 2027**
*Source: EP Legislative Train / Consilium / Covington — committees cleared June 2, plenary vote pending June 2026*
The IMCO and LIBE committees approved the May 7 provisional agreement; Annex III high-risk duties (covering AI used in student/learner assessment) are set to be pushed to December 2027, while new prohibitions on AI-generated CSAM/non-consensual imagery take effect Dec 2, 2026 and the synthetic-content labelling grace period is cut to 3 months.
**Why it matters:** Directly reverses yesterday's most urgent item — the 52-day EU compliance scramble is now an ~18-month runway, freeing roadmap capacity, but the new content-safety prohibitions land first and still bind any UGC or generative feature.

**2. PNAS Nexus: leading AI models fail the classic Stroop attention test**
*Source: PNAS Nexus (Patel, Wang & Fan) / EurekAlert / ScienceDaily — June 10, 2026*
Researchers scaled the color-word Stroop task across multimodal LLMs and accuracy collapsed from >90% to near-failure as conflict load rose, arguing transformers lack human-like executive/conflict-resolution attention control (primary paper tested GPT-4o and Claude 3.5 Sonnet).
**Why it matters:** Fresh, citable evidence that human executive-attention measurement captures something LLMs cannot replicate — a scientific moat argument for game-based cognitive assessment and a caution against trusting LLMs to score or simulate attention tasks.

**3. Apple unveils device-level child-safety suite at WWDC, explicitly inspired by Australia's under-16 rules**
*Source: Apple Newsroom / MacTech / Deseret — June 8–10, 2026*
Apple previewed parental approval of websites, per-app time limits for children, communication approvals, and a redesigned Screen Time; Tim Cook tied the changes to Australia's under-16 framework and PM Albanese publicly welcomed them after a briefing.
**Why it matters:** Platform-level parental controls and per-app time caps are becoming the default OS environment any child-facing cognitive game must live inside — design for parental approval flows and time-limit interoperability now.

**4. Texas App Store Accountability Act takes effect — age verification and parental consent for minors' downloads**
*Source: Texas Tribune / Wiley / Biometric Update — Fifth Circuit stay June 4, rollout coverage June 9–10*
SB 2420 now requires app stores to verify age and obtain parental consent for minors on new Texas accounts, with developer penalties; a constitutional challenge continues but the law is live.
**Why it matters:** Age-gating moves up to the app-store layer in a major US state, adding a distribution checkpoint and a likely template other states copy — affects install funnel and onboarding for any under-18 cognitive game.

**5. 5W EdTech AI Visibility Index: Duolingo and Khan Academy dominate AI-engine citations; legacy SEO winners fall off**
*Source: 5W PR / PR Newswire / Morningstar — June 10, 2026*
The inaugural index ranked how generative engines (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) surface edtech brands; the top 10 captured 71% of inbound institutional sales inquiries on 40% of category marketing spend, while Chegg, Course Hero and Quizlet dropped out of the top 15.
**Why it matters:** Discovery is shifting from search engines to AI answer engines — a cognitive gaming startup's "AI share of voice" is becoming a measurable growth lever, and incumbents are already optimising for it.

**7. Nature Neuroscience: cerebellar "reserve" supports cognitive resilience in ~47,000 adults**
*Source: Nature Neuroscience — ~June 10, 2026*
Across three neuroimaging cohorts, cerebellar aging was spatially heterogeneous and greater cerebellar volume tracked with higher late-life cognitive scores, proposed as a brain-reserve/resilience mechanism.
**Why it matters:** Adds large-sample weight to the "cognitive reserve" framing that underpins brain-training market claims — useful scientific scaffolding for positioning cognitive games as resilience-building rather than mere entertainment.

*Source: Anthropic / CNBC / TechCrunch / Techmeme — June 9–11, 2026*

**Why it matters:** Frontier models can now spin up simple playable game content on demand — accelerating prototyping but commoditising basic task formats, so defensibility shifts further toward validated psychometrics and proprietary data.
