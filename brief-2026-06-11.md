## Top 10 Developments

**1. Cambridge Cognition + Oura: CANTAB cognitive assessments go into 45,000-person wearable study**
*Source: Investegate RNS / Cambridge Network / Proactive Investors — May 28, 2026*
Cambridge Cognition launched a large-scale IRB-approved brain health study integrating CANTAB Pathway into the Oura app; up to 45,000 Oura Ring members will complete in-app cognitive tasks while the ring continuously captures sleep, activity, and physiological data; rollout began May 28 via Oura Labs.
**Why it matters:** The leading clinical cognitive assessment company is consumer-scaling through a 45M-user wearable platform — validates the cognitive assessment + health data fusion model and signals where the market is heading; watch for direct competition or a partnership playbook.

---

**2. McGill/INHANCE RCT: BrainHQ training raises acetylcholine 2.3% in older adults**
*Source: BrainHQ / NBC News / McGill Newsroom — May 31–June 2026*
A 10-week NIH-funded RCT (n=92, 65+) using PET imaging showed BrainHQ exercises nearly offset the natural 2.5%/decade cholinergic decline; the first study directly linking cognitive games to measurable neurochemical change.
**Why it matters:** Gold-standard clinical evidence for the core market claim; directly citable in fundraising decks, NHS/Medicare reimbursement bids, and enterprise wellness pitches.

---

**3. ACTIVE Trial 20-year follow-up: ~23 hrs of speed training → 25% lower dementia risk**
*Source: NIH / Johns Hopkins Medicine / Alzheimer's & Dementia — February 2026, peak news cycle*
Longest-ever cognitive training efficacy dataset, covered by CNN, NBC, and NPR; original ACTIVE trial participants followed for 20 years.
**Why it matters:** Shifts the product narrative from "brain game" to "preventive health intervention" — a more defensible and reimbursable category; provides investment- and regulatory-grade longitudinal evidence.

**4. UK Parliament: government signals under-16 gaming platform bans (June 8 Commons debate)**
*Source: Hansard / House of Commons Library CBP-10468 — June 8, 2026*
Urgent Question debate in the Commons; minister Kanishka Narayan indicated gaming platforms enabling stranger communication may face the same under-16 restrictions as social media; the Children's Wellbeing and Schools Act 2026 (Royal Assent April 29) already creates a statutory duty to impose age/functionality restrictions on gaming for under-16s; "Growing Up in the Online World" consultation closed May 26 with results incoming.
**Why it matters:** Directly threatens any cognitive gaming product marketed at under-16s in the UK — but creates a regulatory moat for compliant, educationally-positioned platforms; age-gating infrastructure becomes a competitive differentiator overnight.

**5. HireVue launches Assessment Builder + acquires Hireguide agentic AI**
*Source: GlobeNewswire / HireVue — February 19 and March 10, 2026*
HireVue launched Assessment Builder (AI-generated, validated role-specific hiring assessments) on Feb 19, then acquired Hireguide's agentic AI technology on March 10 to build a voice-based AI Interviewer that qualifies candidates adaptively before video interviews.
**Why it matters:** The dominant gamified hiring platform is accelerating fast; precision cognitive-style assessment is going mass-market in HR; both a competitive threat and a validation that the corporate assessment market will spend.

**7. EU AI Act: learner assessment AI classified “high-risk” — August 2, 2026 deadline (52 days)**
*Source: Holland & Knight / EU Digital Strategy — April–June 2026*
Hard deadline for transparency, human oversight, bias audits, and registration for AI systems used in student assessment, admissions, and academic monitoring; a May 7 political agreement may defer some provisions but not the “high-risk” classification.
**Why it matters:** 52-day runway to comply or lose EU revenue; non-compliant platforms face existential risk in EU schools and HR; compliant players lock in a durable regulatory moat before the field catches up.

**8. Australia OAIC Children's Online Privacy Code: covers games, consultation closed June 5**
*Source: OAIC / DLA Piper / Corrs — March–June 2026*
Exposure draft published March 31; covers apps, games, and websites used by children (not just social media); key provisions: best-interests-of-child test before data collection; consent for targeted advertising; right to deletion; geolocation notification; Code registration targeted December 10, 2026.
**Why it matters:** AU cognitive gaming products targeting children face new compliance requirements within months; data architecture and consent flow decisions made now determine whether AU market is accessible at launch.
